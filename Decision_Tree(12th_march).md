I explored the concept of decision trees by creating a simple example to decide whether it is a good idea to play outside or not
A decision tree is a machine learning model that makes decisions by asking a sequence of questions and following different branches based on the answers. 
Each question represents a condition, and the final outcome is reached after evaluating these conditions step by step.
For this example, I considered factors such as weather conditions and temperature to decide whether playing outside would be suitable. 
The first decision is based on whether it is raining or not. If it is raining, the decision is not to play outside. 
If it is not raining, the next condition checks the temperature. If the temperature is too hot, it may still not be comfortable to play outside. 
However, if the weather is pleasant, the decision is to play outside.

Example Decision Logic:
Check if it is raining.
If yes ->Do not play outside.
If no -> Check temperature.
Check temperature.
If it is very hot → Do not play outside.
If it is pleasant → Play outside.
