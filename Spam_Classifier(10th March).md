Features Used for Spam Detection
1.To detect spam emails, the system can look at several features. These may include the presence of suspicious words such as “free,” “win,” or “offer,”
the number of links included in the email, the reputation of the sender, and the number of exclamation marks used in the message. 
The overall length of the email and the frequency of promotional keywords can also be useful indicators.
These features help the model understand patterns that are commonly found in spam messages.
2.Data Needed to Train the Model
For a spam classifier to work effectively, it needs a dataset containing many emails that are already labeled as spam or not spam. 
This dataset helps the model learn the differences between normal communication emails and spam messages. 
By analyzing this labeled data, the model learns patterns and improves its ability to correctly classify new incoming emails.
3.Possible Mistakes in Spam Detection
Even though spam detection systems are useful, they can sometimes make mistakes. One common mistake is a false positive, where a normal email is 
mistakenly marked as spam. For example, an important message from a bank or a workplace email might end up in the spam folder. Another possible mistake 
is a false negative, where a spam email is not detected and appears in the inbox.
Because of these possibilities, spam detection systems need continuous improvement and better training data.
